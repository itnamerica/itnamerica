Member Programs

Every time an older person stops driving, a local business runs the risk of losing a customer. ITN{{affiliate}} offers innovative programs that both fight isolation and keep customers coming through the door of community businesses.

CarTrade™

The CarTrade™ program allows seniors to make use of an unused car and exchange the value of the car for ride credits.

Receive a possible tax deduction or ITN ride credits for yourself or others when you donate or trade your car.

Find out how you can trade you car by contacting us

Gift Certificate

Friends and family can give you a lift by purchasing ITN gift certificates for birthdays, Mother's Day, or any occasion.

A gift certificate for transportation is an original gift that can be used to take fun trips or necessary ones. They are a great way to help your parent or friend fund their Personal Transportation Account™. Celebrate Mother's Day, birthdays, and special holidays with a gift certificate in any amount, sent in a beautiful card by ITN{{affiliate}}.
Call or e-mail to order.

If you are trying to encourage safe, alternative transportation for your loved one, consider a gift certificate to pay for membership and their first ride. Our trained drivers will transport your loved one with special care -- opening doors, carrying packages, and offering friendly assistance as needed.

Ride & Shop

The Ride & Shop™ program brings paying customers right to the door, then helps them with their packages when they leave.

ITN riders can receive ride discounts when they patronize these businesses.

HealthyMiles

HealthyMiles ensures patients can get to your office, whether you’re a doctor, physical therapist, chiropractor or massage therapist.

ITN riders can receive ride discounts to these businesses.

Road Scholarship™

The Road Scholarship Program is primarily funded by volunteers who donate transportation credits earned when they drive others. Eligibility standards are set locally, and riders who meet the standards are eligible for a scholarship to help cover some of the cost of their rides.

Community Road Scholarship™

Volunteers donate credits to a fund for a municipality, town or other government agency or for an organization fund for their citizens or members, such as the City of Portland or AARP, local or national.

Personal Transportation Account™

The Personal Transportation Account™ is one of the core business innovations of the ITN system because it allows an ITN member to store transportation assets in various forms - either as a credit or as cash. Credits may be earned as a volunteer driver, by trading a car for rides through the CarTrade™ program, as a birthday credit, or as a referral credit.

Cash may come from pre-payment, gift certificates, co-payments from participating merchants and healthcare providers, or from Ride Services accounts. ITN is a cashless system. Seniors who ride receive a statement once a month detailing the rides they have taken. Volunteer drivers also have personal transportation accounts to store their credits and receive statements detailing their accumulated balances.

Transportation Social Security™

Independent Transportation Network ® (ITN) volunteer drivers store transportation credits for their volunteer efforts. These credits may be used to plan for their own future transit needs or they may use these credits to help pay for rides for members of their family or for low income seniors through the Road Scholarship Program™. ITN transportation credits are honored at any ITN in the country.

Ride Services

Community organizations such as churches, assisted living facilities, and health care providers may contract with ITN to provide transportation services for their members. Once a month, ITN sends a statement to the organization to pay for the rides that the members take. This is a way for community organizations to supplement the transportation services they already provide. Community organizations that participate in Ride Services also help to support the ITN affiliate organization.
